ADA staking is interesting because you choose which validator (pool) to support. You have more control than Binance staking. Let me walk you through it.

## ADA Staking vs Binance Staking

**Binance staking**:
- Easy, just click
- Less control
- You trust Binance

**ADA staking (direct)**:
- Slightly more involved
- Full control (choose pool)
- Decentralized (true crypto staking)
- Learn how blockchain works

ADA staking teaches you how crypto actually works.

## Getting a Wallet

You need an ADA-compatible wallet. Options:

**Daedalus** (Official):
- Most secure
- Full node (heavy, ~10GB)
- Best for serious ADA holders

**Yoroi** (Recommended):
- Lightweight browser extension
- Easiest to use
- Still secure
- Best for beginners

**Ledger/Trezor**:
- Hardware wallet
- Most secure
- Best for large amounts

For most people: Yoroi is perfect.

## Step 1: Download Yoroi Wallet

1. Go to yoroi-wallet.com
2. Download extension for your browser (Chrome, Firefox, etc.)
3. Install it
4. Create new wallet
   - Click "Create wallet"
   - Choose Cardano
   - Set password (strong!)
   - Save recovery phrase (CRITICAL)

## Step 2: Send ADA to Your Wallet

1. Get your Cardano wallet address from Yoroi
   - Click your wallet
   - See "Address" section
   - Copy address

2. Send ADA from Binance (or other exchange):
   - Go to Withdraw
   - Select ADA
   - Paste your Yoroi address
   - Confirm

Wait for transfer (usually 5-10 minutes).

## Step 3: Delegate to Stake Pool

Once ADA is in wallet:

1. Open Yoroi
2. Click "Delegation"
3. Search for stake pool
4. Pool shows: Name, APY, Pool size, Fee
5. Click pool
6. Click "Delegate"
7. Confirm delegation

That's it. You're now staking.

## Choosing a Stake Pool

This is where decentralization matters. You choose validator.

What to look for:

**APY**: Higher is better (3.5%-5% is good)
- If pool claims 10%+ APY, it's lying or unsustainable

**Pool Size**: Larger usually better
- Fewer rewards if too large (diluted)
- Risk if too small (might fail)
- Sweet spot: 50-200M ADA

**Saturation**: What percent full is pool?
- If 100% saturated, new delegates earn less
- Better to pick non-saturated

**Fee**: How much pool takes (usually 0-3%)
- Lower is better
- 2% is normal

**Uptime**: Does pool have good track record?
- Check their stats
- 99%+ uptime is good

**Community**: Is there an active community?
- Ask questions on their Discord
- Responsive team is good sign

## Popular Stake Pools

Some well-known pools:

- **TLPL** (Tony Lake): Charity-focused, good APY
- **CCLB** (Coffee Club): Community-driven, active team
- **BUFFY**: No-fee pool (they rely on tips)
- **ADACT**: Large, reliable

Research before choosing. Delegation is flexible—you can change anytime.

## Rewards Timeline

**Day 1**: You delegate
- Nothing yet

**Epoch 1** (~5 days): Waiting
- Your ADA counted for next epoch
- Still no rewards

**Epoch 2** (~5 days): First reward
- You receive first ADA reward
- Happens automatically

**Ongoing**: Weekly rewards
- Every epoch (~5 days), you get new ADA
- Automatic deposits to wallet

Total wait: 2-3 weeks for first reward.

## Calculating Rewards

At 4% APY:

- 100 ADA: Earn 4 ADA/year (~0.3 ADA/week)
- 1,000 ADA: Earn 40 ADA/year (~3 ADA/week)
- 10,000 ADA: Earn 400 ADA/year (~30 ADA/week)

Not huge, but real passive income.

## Rewards Frequency

Rewards come in during specific epochs. You'll see:

- New ADA appears in wallet periodically
- Not daily (it's batched)
- Weekly is common
- Shows in wallet as "Rewards"

You can see reward history in Yoroi (Shows when, how much).

## Unstaking

If you want to stop staking:

1. Open Yoroi
2. Go to Delegation
3. Click "Undelegate"
4. Confirm
5. Your ADA is immediately unstaked

No lock-up period. Full flexibility. Rewards stop at next epoch.

## Keeping Rewards and Restaking

Your rewards automatically accrue in wallet. You can:

**Option 1**: Redelegate
- New ADA from rewards joins your stake
- Automatic compound growth

**Option 2**: Withdraw rewards
- Take earned ADA out of wallet
- Use for something else

Most people redelegate (let it compound).

## Switching Pools

If you don't like your current pool:

1. Open Yoroi
2. Click your delegated pool
3. Click "Redelegate" or undelegate
4. Choose new pool
5. Delegate to new pool

Process takes 2 epochs (~10 days) to take effect. No penalty.

## Security When Staking

**Best practices**:
- Use strong password
- Save recovery phrase safely (offline, not email)
- Don't share recovery phrase
- Verify wallet address before receiving ADA
- Use official wallet site (yoroi-wallet.com, not phishing)

Staking doesn't increase security risk—your coins stay in your wallet.

## Tax on ADA Staking

When you earn ADA staking:

- Taxable as ordinary income (in US)
- Tax at your income rate (up to 37%)
- Due even if you don't sell
- Must track for taxes

Example: Earn 50 ADA/year
- If ADA is $0.50, that's $25 income
- You owe 10%-37% depending on bracket

Set aside for taxes.

## Common Questions

**Can I lose my ADA?**
No. Staking doesn't risk your coins. Worst case: poor APY from bad pool choice. Undelegate and redelegate to better pool.

**Is there minimum ADA?**
No. Stake 1 ADA if you want (rewards are tiny but no minimum).

**Is there maximum?**
Technically no, but pools have saturation limits. Very large stakes might need multiple delegations.

**Can I delegate to multiple pools?**
Yes, split your ADA across pools. Some people do this for decentralization.
